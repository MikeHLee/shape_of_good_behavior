\begin{abstract}
As autonomous systems are deployed in complex environments, ensuring behavioral safety becomes critical. Standard Safe Reinforcement Learning methods like Constrained Policy Optimization (CPO) use expectation-based constraints that allow for catastrophic tail events, particularly when deceptive rewards overwhelm soft penalties. In this paper, we introduce a geometric approach to behavioral safety based on Conformal Riemannian Metrics and Sheaf-Geodesic Policy Optimization (SGPO). By defining dangerous states as geometric singularities (``behavioral black holes'') where the conformal factor σ(x) → ∞, we render unsafe regions geodesically unreachable by any policy update. In our ``Murky Drone'' and ``Agentic Shortcut'' scenarios, we demonstrate that baselines like PPO and CPO violate safety constraints 100\% of the time, whereas SGPO consistently achieves 0\% safety violations by leveraging the infinite geodesic distance to catastrophic regions.
\end{abstract}
